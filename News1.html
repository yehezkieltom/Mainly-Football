Peckham Town football match called off after man 'broke player's jaw with punch on pitch'

An amateur football match was abandoned after a player's jaw was broken as he was allegedly punched by an opponent on the pitch.

Peckham Town midfielder Ali Abdullahi, 25, suffered a broken jaw and a fractured eye socket when he was reportedly punched during the club’s Kent County League clash with Stansfeld Oxford and Bermondsey on Saturday.

He was discharged from King's College Hospital on Tuesday having undergone surgery to repair his jaw and is recovering at home where family said he remains in “distress”.

Peckham Town chairman and founder Bryan Hall told the Standard his side’s opponents arrived at Pyners Close playing fields “completely pumped up” for the match.

He said: “In the 27th minute the referee awarded us the free kick and some of their players kicked off about it.

Describing the moment Mr Abdullahi was hit, Mr Hall added: “He collapsed immediately. Our physios rushed on tried to get him off the pitch and he collapsed again so we knew how serious it was.

“There is no place for this in football. We don’t have any history with this group we are just here to play football.”

Mr Hall said Peckham players spent hours at Mr Abdullahi’s bedside at Lewisham Hospital following the attack and that he received “thousands” of supportive messages since it took place.

He added: “He was a mess. He can’t speak. It was a sad sight. He is a gentle person. He is a big strong boy but he is quietly spoken.”

Father-of-two Mr Abdullahi, who used to play for neighbours Dulwich Hamlet, is a self-employed removals man who was due to start work this week.

Speaking from the family home, his wife, who did not want to be named, told the Standard: “Alli would like to thank everyone who has shown support to him. The incident has left him in distress. Alli is a very gentle person, he is softly spoken so he can’t believe this has happened to him.

“We are just shocked that this kind of behaviour can take place on a football pitch.”

An online fundraising page set up by Dulwich Hamlet Supporters' Trust to support Mr Abdullahi as he recovers from his injuries has raised nearly £500.

Stansfeld Oxford and Bermondsey chairman Ian Rooney said the club was unable to comment on the incident until it had received the FA’s match report, which is due imminently.

A Met police spokesman confirmed a 24-year-old man was arrested on suspicion of GBH.
